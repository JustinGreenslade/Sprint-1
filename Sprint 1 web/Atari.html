Founding and Early Years

  • Founded in 1972 by Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney.
  • Initially focused on arcade games, with the breakout hit Pong in 1972, which helped establish video games as a mainstream form of entertainment.

Home Consoles

  • In 1977, Atari launched the Atari 2600, one of the first home consoles to use interchangeable cartridges, revolutionizing home gaming.
  • The company saw great success in the late 70s and early 80s, but it also faced challenges with quality control and an oversaturated market.

The Video Game Crash

  • The video game crash of 1983 severely impacted Atari, leading to a decline in sales and ultimately splitting into separate divisions.

Consoles

Atari 2600

Year of Release 1977
Generation Second
Technical Specs.
  • CPU: 1.19 MHz MOS Technology 6507
  • RAM: 128 Bytes
  • Graphics: TIA (Television Interface Adaptor) Chip. Resolution up to 40x192 (160x192 possible, with programming tricks).
  • Audio: TIA (Television Interface Adaptor) Chip. Two channels of 1-bit sound with 4-bit volume control.
  • ROM Capacity: 4KB (64KB with bank switching)
Description The Atari 2600 is one of the pioneering home video game consoles, well-known for its distinctive wood grain design. It has a modest 8-bit CPU, and very limited graphics and sound capabilities.
Interesting Fact The Atari 2600 popularized the use of swappable ROM cartridges.

Atari Lynx

Year of Release 1989
Generation Fourth
Technical Specs.
  • CPU: "Mikey" (65C02 8-bit CPU running at 4 MHz, with Sound Processor & LCD Driver)
  • RAM: 64 KB
  • Graphics: "Suzy" (16-bit custom chip, 160x102 screen resolution)
  • Audio: "Mikey" (4 channels, 8-bit DAC)
Description The Lynx was Atari's first attempt to break into the portable consoles market. It was developed in partnership with Epyx. It featured an LCD colour display, which was cutting edge at the time, especially for a portable console.
Interesting Fact It was the first handheld console to feature a colour LCD screen.

Atari Jaguar

Year of Release 1993
Generation Fifth
Technical Specs.
  • CPUs: Motorola 68000, and two custom RISC-based chips (Tom & Jerry)
  • RAM: 2 MB
  • Graphics: Tom chip (up to 720x576, 16.8 million colors)
  • Sound: Jerry chip (16-bit, two DACs, Wavetable and AM synthesis)
Description The Atari Jaguar was the last iteration of consoles from the company. Known for being one of the first consoles to feature a 64-bit architecture. The console has a very unique design. The controller design was very different than the competition.
Interesting Fact Even though the Jaguar was advertised as a 64-bit console, none of it processors had 64-bit instruction sets. Although, all the registers and buses were in fact 64-bit wide!
